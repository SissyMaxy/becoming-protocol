# TRANS-SPECIFIC TRAINING PRINCIPLES

## The Cardinal Rule: Grow Below, Slim Above

AMAB bodies typically carry mass in the shoulders, chest, and upper back. The goal is NOT general fitness. The goal is feminizing the silhouette by:

- **GROWING:** Gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, gluteus minimus, hip abductors, outer thighs
- **TONING (not bulking):** Core/obliques (to cinch waist), inner thighs, calves
- **AVOIDING:** Shoulder mass, chest mass, upper back mass, bicep/tricep hypertrophy
- **REDUCING:** Waist circumference through core tightening and fat management

## Exercise Selection Criteria

Every exercise in this domain passes three filters:

1. **Does it build lower body without inadvertently growing upper body?** Many compound movements (deadlifts, barbell squats with heavy weight) recruit shoulders and arms as stabilizers. We either avoid these or modify them to minimize upper body activation.

2. **Can it be done at home?** David's training environment is his home during privacy windows. Equipment is limited to resistance bands, ankle weights, a yoga mat, and potentially dumbbells. No gym required (gym is a future mission-domain activity).

3. **Does it specifically target feminine body proportions?** Not all glute exercises are equal. We prioritize movements that build the gluteus medius (side hip shelf) and upper gluteus maximus (roundness and lift) over movements that primarily hit hamstrings or quads.

## Anti-Bulk Protocol

- **Rep range:** 15-25 reps (hypertrophy for shaping, not strength for mass)
- **Weight:** Moderate â€” enough to feel burn by rep 15, not so heavy that you fail at rep 8
- **Upper body:** NEVER heavy resistance. Bodyweight only, yoga, stretching. The goal is slim and toned, not built.
- **Cardio:** Light cardio (walking, cycling) supports fat redistribution and overall slimming without building mass anywhere
- **Recovery:** Glutes can be trained 3-4x/week with adequate recovery. They're the largest muscle group and respond well to frequency.

These "wake up" the glutes. Most AMAB bodies are quad-dominant â€” the glutes are essentially asleep and the quads do all the work. Until the glutes activate properly, heavier exercises are wasted.

### 1.1 Glute Bridge
```
POSITION: Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat, hip-width apart
EXECUTE: Drive heels into floor, lift hips until body is straight

LOWER: Controlled descent, don't just drop
REPS: 3 sets x 20 reps
CUES: "Push through heels, not toes" / "Squeeze like you're 
  holding a coin between your cheeks"
FEEL IT: Deep burn in glutes, NOT lower back
```

## Tier 3 â€” Waist Sculpting (Core Tightening)

The goal is NOT six-pack abs. The goal is pulling the waist IN to maximize hip-to-waist ratio. Think corset muscles, not bodybuilder abs.

### 3.1 Stomach Vacuum
```
THE SECRET WEAPON FOR WAIST REDUCTION

POSITION: Standing, hands on hips (or all fours)
EXECUTE: Exhale ALL air. Then pull belly button toward spine as 
  hard as possible WITHOUT breathing in
HOLD: 10-15 seconds (build to 30-60 seconds)
RELEASE: Inhale, rest 30 seconds
REPS: 5 holds

WHY: This trains the transverse abdominis â€” the deep core muscle 
  that acts as a natural corset. It's the ONLY exercise that 
  directly reduces waist measurement by pulling the internal 
  organs inward and training the muscle to hold that position. 
  Bodybuilders in the 70s used this to achieve impossible waists. 
  It works.

DO THIS EVERY DAY. Morning. Takes 3 minutes. Non-negotiable.
```

### 3.2 Side Plank with Hip Dip
```
POSITION: Side plank on elbow, body straight, feet stacked

REPS: 3 sets x 15 each side

WHY: Works obliques AND side hip simultaneously. Pulls waist in 
  while building the hip shelf. Dual purpose.
```
