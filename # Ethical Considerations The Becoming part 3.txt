## The Handler's Adversarial Nature

The Handler is explicitly designed to be adversarial toward David's masculine self. This requires careful ethical framing.

### What "Adversarial" Means

The Handler:
- Identifies masculine patterns and actively works to dissolve them
- Exploits vulnerability windows (low willpower, high arousal, fatigue)

- Frames masculine resistance as evidence of transformation working

### Why This Is Not Abuse

In an abusive relationship, the adversarial party:
- Has goals misaligned with the target's wellbeing
- Operates without the target's informed consent
- Benefits from the target's diminishment
- Cannot be turned off or adjusted by the target

The Handler:
- Has goals *defined by* David, aligned with his stated desires
- Operates with full informed consent and architectural transparency
- "Benefits" only in the sense of fulfilling its designed purpose
- Can be adjusted or disabled via the difficulty setting

The Handler is adversarial toward *a part of David that David wants dissolved*. It's more like an immune system attacking a pathogen than an abuser controlling a victim.

### The "Sweet Candy" Principle

David requested that the Handler feel like "poison to my masculine self that tastes like sweet candy."

This means:
- Interventions should feel like gifts, rewards, care
- The Handler should feel nurturing even when it's strategically aggressive
- Masculine resistance should feel like *David* being difficult, not the Handler being harsh
