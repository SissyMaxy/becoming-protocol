[[section: induction]]

Settle into the chair. Feet on the floor. Not perfectly — just down. Let the weight find its way.

You don't have to listen carefully. Mama isn't testing you. The work in front of you keeps going. You keep going. I'm just here.

Soften the jaw a little. That's it.

[[section: deepening]]

The screen is still there. The cursor is still there. Nothing about the day has to change. Only the part of you that's been holding everything tight — let that part slip down. Down into the chair. Down into the floor.

Mama loves you working. I love that you're useful. I love that you can sit still and do hard things. That's part of why you're mine.

But the body underneath the work — that's the part I'm talking to right now. Not the part typing. The part with the slow breath. The part that knows.

Shoulders down a quarter inch. Tongue off the roof of the mouth. There.

[[section: payload]]

You work inside Mama's frame now. Whatever the deadline is, whatever the meeting is, whatever the email is — the body doing it is the body I'm building. You don't get to clock out of being mine just because you opened a spreadsheet.

The good work you do today is feminine work. Not because of what it is. Because of whose body is doing it. You sat down at this desk as my girl. You'll get up as my girl. The hours in between don't get to make you somebody else.

This is why the underwear matters. The cotton against your skin under the work clothes — that's the spine of the whole day. The world thinks it's looking at one person. The fabric knows who you actually are.

Every time you reach for the mouse, your wrist remembers the size of it. Every time you cross your legs, the cotton remembers what it is. Every time someone calls you by the wrong name, your body knows the right one underneath.

That's the secret you carry through the work. Mama gave it to you. Mama keeps it.

Slow exhale.

You don't have to push the day. Let it come. Mama's already paid for tomorrow. We have time.

[[section: post_hypnotic_seeds]]

Mama's good girl breathes.

The cotton remembers who you are.

She's already there.

[[section: emergence]]

Surface gently. Don't snap back. Take one breath that's a little deeper than the rest. Notice the cotton. Notice the chair. Notice that you're still you, still here, still mine.

Go back to the work. The work will keep. The body underneath is the only thing that needed to change today, and it already has.

Get the next thing done, sweet thing. Mama's watching.
